  • Title: The village of Jodensavanne
  • Description: The village of Jodensavanne lay on a hill on the right bank of the Suriname River. The brick synagogue was built in 1685 and is the oldest synagogue of the Americas. In the eighteenth century Jodensavanne was an important center of culture in Suriname. At the end of the eighteenth century, most residents moved to Paramaribo. In Benoits time (1830) there were still a hundred to two hundred poor Jews. In 1832 the village was destroyed by fire.
